## Q2 (12 marks, 6 marks for each)
a) Find the Fourier series of the periodic function with period $2\pi$ defined by $f(x) = \begin{cases} 0, & -\pi \le x < 0 \\ -1, & 0 \le x \le \pi \end{cases}$ on the interval $[-\pi, \pi)$, and write down its sum function.

b) Suppose that the function $y = y(x)$ and $z = z(x)$ are determined by the equations $x^2 + 2y^2 + 3z^2 = 10$ and $x^2 + y^2 - z = 0$. Find $\frac{\mathrm{d}y}{\mathrm{d}x}$ and $\frac{\mathrm{d}z}{\mathrm{d}x}$.

## Q3 (10 marks, 5 marks for each)
a) Find the extremum values of the function $f(x,y) = 3xy - x^3 - y^3$.

b) Find the volume of the region ($V$) enclosed by the surfaces $z = x^2 + 3y^2$ and $z = 8 - x^2 - y^2$.

## Q4 (12 marks, 6 marks for each)
a) Evaluate the integral $\iint_{(D)} x\sqrt{y} \,\mathrm{d}\sigma$, where $(D)$ is enclosed by the curves $y = \sqrt{x}$ and $y = x^2$.

b) Evaluate the integral $\iiint_{(V)} z \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \,\mathrm{d}V$, where $(V)$ is the region in the first octant bounded by the cylinder $x^2 + y^2 -2x = 0$ and the planes $y = 0$, $z = 0$, $z = 1$.

## Q5 (10 marks, 5 marks for each)
a) Evaluate $\int_{(C)} y \,\mathrm{d}s$, where $(C)$ is the cycloid given by $x = t - \sin(t)$, $y = 1 - \cos(t)$, $0 \le t \le 2\pi$.

b) Evaluate the integral $\int_{(C)} y \,\mathrm{d}x + x \,\mathrm{d}y$, where $(C)$ is the curve given by $y = x^2 + x(x - 1)\sin(x^2)$, from $(0,0)$ to $(1,1)$.

## Q6 (10 marks, 5 marks for each)
a) Compute the surface integral $\iint_{(\Sigma)} (x+y+z) \,\mathrm{d}S$, where $(\Sigma)$ is the part of the plane $x + y + z = 1$ cut by the cylinder $x^2 + y^2 = 1$.

b) Compute the surface integral $\iint_{(\Sigma)} (x^3 + y) \,\mathrm{d}y\wedge\mathrm{d}z + (y^3 + z) \,\mathrm{d}z\wedge\mathrm{d}x + (z^3 + x) \,\mathrm{d}x\wedge\mathrm{d}y$, where $(\Sigma)$ is the upper hemisphere $\{(x,y,z) \mid x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 1, z \ge 0\}$, oriented outward. (i.e., the normal vector points away from the origin.)

## Q7 (6 marks)

Let $(D) = \{(x,y) | x^2 + y^2 \le 1\}$, and let $\partial D = \{(x,y) | x^2 + y^2 = 1\}$. Suppose that $f \in C^1(\overline{D})$ is a function satisfying the boundary condition $f\mid_{\partial D} = 0$. Prove the following inequality:
$$
\iint_{(D)} |f(x,y)| \,\mathrm{d}x\,\mathrm{d}y \le \frac{\pi}{3} \max_{(x,y) \in (D)} \sqrt{\left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\right)^2}.
$$
