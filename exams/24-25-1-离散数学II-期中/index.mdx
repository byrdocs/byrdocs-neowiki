## 1.

Find $7^{602} \mod 61$.

## 2.

Find all solutions, if any, to the system of congruences

$$
\begin{cases}
x \equiv 3 (\mathrm{mod} 7) \\
x \equiv 2 (\mathrm{mod} 5)
\end{cases}
$$

## 3.

(a) How many different strings can be made by reordering the letters of the word MATHEMATICAL?

(b) How many bit strings of length 11 have more 0’s than 1’s?

## 4.

How many ways can be made by distribute hands of 7 cards each to 3 players from a standard deck of 54 cards?

## 5.

(a) Find the number of solutions to $x + y + z = 47$, where $x$, $y$, and $z$ are nonnegative integers.

(b) Answer part (a), but assume that $x \geq 7$ and $y \geq 15$.

## 6.

How many numbers must be selected from the set $\{-1, -2, -3, -4, -5, -6,1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\}$ to guarantee that at least one pair of these numbers add up to 0?

## 7.

Let $Q$ be the set of rational numbers and define $a * b = a + b - ab$

(a) Is $(Q, *)$ a monoid? Justify your answer.

(b) If $(Q, *)$ a monoid, which elements of $Q$ have an inverse?

(c) Is $(Q, *)$ a group? Justify your answer.

## 8.

Consider the monoid $S = \{e, a, b, c\}$ with the following operation table.

| * | e | a | b | c |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **e** | e | a | b | c |
| **a** | a | e | b | c |
| **b** | b | c | b | c |
| **c** | c | b | b | c |

Consider the congruence relation $R = \{(e, a), (a, e), (e, e), (a, a), (b, c), (b, b), (c, b), (c, c)\}$ on $S$.

(a) Write the operation table of the quotient monoid $S/R$.

(b) Describe the natural homomorphism $f_R: S \to S/R$ and give your reason.

## 9.

Consider the group $(\mathbb{Z}_6, +_6)$.

(a) Write the operation table of the group $(\mathbb{Z}_6, +_6)$.

(b) Consider the subgroup $H = \{[0], [3]\}$. Describe the left cosets and right cosets of $H$ in $\mathbb{Z}_6$.

(c) Write the operation table of the quotient group $\mathbb{Z}_6 / H$.

(d) Determine whether the group $(\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_3, \oplus)$ and $(\mathbb{Z}_6, +_6)$ are isomorphic.

## 10.

Let $G$ be a group, and let $H$ and $K$ be the subgroups of $G$. Let the relation $R$ on $G$ be defined as follows:

$aRb$ if and only if $\exists h \in H, \exists k \in K, b = hak$

Show that $R$ is an equivalence relation.
